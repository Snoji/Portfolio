Mood

Summary

Over a 10 week period, our team of 3 worked on developing a web application to help people track and better their emotional well being.

Key Skills

Needfinding • Experience Design • Prototyping • User Testing
Web Dev

My Role

Lead Programmer • Product Designer • Product Manager

Design Brief

Life moves very quickly and your mental health is the most important thing. But with packed schedules, activities, friends, work, and school, there is barely enough time to reflect on your day. You could end the day feeling sad, exhausted, or satisfied, and still be perplexed as to why. Mood attempts to answer this issue. It helps you track your emotions throughout the day by periodically reminding you to journal your feelings, and based off of these entries, suggest activities that put you in a good mood.

Needfinding

We conducted needfinding by creating a list of questions that helped us identify our users through a POV of "stressed college students". In this, we created storyboards that allowed us to empathize with the problem: mental health growth.

Prototyping

We started paper prototyping to create an MVP which allowed us to see the flaws in our designs and led to reiterations. These iterations combined with A/B testing was a means to narrow our broad scope, ultimately defining Mood from wireframes to a branded high fidelity model.

Wireframe

This was our first iteration of our prototype that we used for user testing.

Final Design

After user testing, we noticed that our first prototype had some flaws that we had to address. These included things like having too much functionality and a confusing interface. We also believed we could better visually deisgn the app to fit what we envision the app to be. To address these issues, we cut down on our app functionaliy to three core functions, rebranded the app and UI, and changed textual hierarchy and scaffolding to better indicate sections.
